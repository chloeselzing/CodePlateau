Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, l dd study exotie tribal dances. lo answer your question,
my favourite dances are as follows, with definitions:

Polynesian chicken dance

A little known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC, a whole village would dance around
in a circle like chickens, to encourage their livestock to be "fruitful".


Icelandic brownian shuffle

Before the lcelanders developed fire as a means of getting warm, they used to practice this dance, which
involved hudding close together in a circle on the toor, and shutfing their bodies around in imperceptibly
tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he thought this dance inspired
modern styles such as Twerking.


Arctic robot dance

An interesting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s believed to have
discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic", stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitants
of Northerm Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this
because they were really cold.
